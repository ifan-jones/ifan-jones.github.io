Shop

In 1881, William Williams, a shoemaker lived in Cambrian. Also living here, as a lodger, was William Owens, Talybont, Cardiganshire, who was the village curate. In 1901, John Parry, a church cleric lived in Cambrian. Later, John a Dora Jones owned it (formally of Fron and Hafod). In the 1940, the owner was William Griffith’s father, Mari Fron’s husband.


In front of the shop in at the beginning of the twentieth century. Before 1910, Dora Jones was the owner.
